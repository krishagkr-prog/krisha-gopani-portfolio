Focus Areas

Research Interests

I am interested in building intelligent systems that can perceive, reason, and learn. My focus is on understanding the principles behind modern AI through implementation, experimentation, and continuous exploration.

Large Language Models

Understanding how transformer-based models learn, reason, and generalize through fine-tuning, retrieval augmentation, and systematic evaluation.

Computer Vision

Building visual systems for perception, pose estimation, image understanding, and real-time inference using deep convolutional networks.

Deep Learning

Exploring neural architectures, optimization strategies, representation learning, and the mathematical foundations of modern AI systems.

Multimodal AI

Investigating vision-language models that combine visual and textual signals to enable richer cross-modal understanding and zero-shot capabilities.

AI Agents

Designing autonomous systems that can reason, plan, retrieve knowledge, and use tools to solve complex multi-step problems without human intervention.

Efficient AI Systems

Studying parameter-efficient fine-tuning, model compression, and scalable inference to make powerful AI systems practical and resource-aware.

Who I Am

About

I enjoy understanding, building, and experimenting with modern AI systems — driven by curiosity, an engineering mindset, and a love for solving hard problems.

I am an AI systems builder who approaches every project as an opportunity to understand how things work at a fundamental level. I don't just implement — I experiment, compare approaches, and dig into the math behind models.

My workflow is research-driven: I read papers, reproduce results, and then build on top of them. Whether it's training a transformer from scratch, fine-tuning an LLM, or designing a multi-agent pipeline, I care about understanding the principles, not just achieving the output.

I am continuously learning, continuously experimenting, and genuinely excited about the direction AI research is heading — especially at the intersection of reasoning, retrieval, and multimodal understanding.
